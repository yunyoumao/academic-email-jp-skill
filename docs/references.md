## Design Notes

- Academic email quality depends on relationship, request burden, and context, not only on keigo.
- Default output should be the email draft itself, without assistant prefaces or self-evaluation.
- High-burden requests need concrete information packaging: purpose, deadline, submission method, and attached materials.
- Administrative or report-submission emails should preserve factual identifiers such as course name, student ID, deadline, and attachment status when provided by the user.
- Lab-visit and first-contact emails should mention research fit concretely, but must not invent publications, meetings, or affiliations.
